# Reforger-First Adaptation Guide

## Purpose

This document resets Campaign Juniper Spear 2026 as a terrain-first ARMA Reforger campaign instead of a literal Somalia conversion.

The design goal is to preserve the original campaign spine while adapting it to a modern RHS-based combined-arms experience that fits Reforger mission scale, terrain limits, and player counts.

## Design pillars

1. Keep the campaign spine, not the exact geography.
2. Choose terrain based on gameplay capability first.
3. Build missions for platoon and company execution, not for full brigade simulation.
4. Let briefings, radio traffic, and background activity represent the wider battalion fight.
5. Preserve the WMD hunt, coastal entry, urban fights, inland pursuit, and final HVT encirclement.

## Theater reframing

### Narrative frame

Juniper Spear is re-set in a fictional Horn-of-Africa-inspired theater centered on the **Hadir Littoral** and the **Daryan Interior**.

- **Hadir Littoral** is the coastal battlespace: port facilities, an international airfield, sprawling peri-urban districts, industrial zones, and a dense city core.
- **Daryan Interior** is the pursuit battlespace: open scrubland, broken desert, scattered villages, militia camps, highway choke points, and a cross-border sanctuary network.

This retains the feel of an expeditionary intervention against an extremist-pirate network without forcing one-to-one Somalia mapping.

### Threat framing

- **Primary HVT:** Ahmad Umar
- **Network:** extremist militia with pirate facilitators, black-market brokers, and defected military personnel
- **Strategic threat:** movement and concealment of chemical or radiological material
- **Operational pattern:** use the coast for smuggling, the city for concealment, and the interior for dispersal and survival

## Terrain package

### Terrain selection rule

Every chosen terrain must support repeatable combined-arms mission making before it is considered narratively valid.

### Required terrain features

#### Primary terrain: coastal maneuver terrain

Must include:

- a port or coastal insertion area
- an airfield suitable for forcible entry or sustainment
- one dense urban district
- multiple peri-urban approaches
- road network that supports convoy and mechanized operations
- enough depth for helicopter movement and alternate landing zones

This terrain hosts the opening lodgment, the first urban fights, the rescue mission, and the major breakthrough.

#### Secondary terrain: inland pursuit terrain

Must include:

- long movement corridors for mounted sweeps
- sparse settlements suitable for raid or cordon operations
- open flight lanes for air assault and CASEVAC
- choke points and crossing sites
- remote compounds or camp networks

This terrain hosts the pursuit, camp destruction, WMD recovery, and HVT isolation missions.

#### Optional finale terrain: border-city objective

If a third terrain is used, it should provide:

- one contained urban objective
- industrial or administrative compounds
- layered defensive belts
- clean boundaries for encirclement

If a third terrain is not available, the finale should be built as a contained district on the secondary terrain.

## Faction package

### Friendly force

The campaign should use a modern RHS-based task force centered on a reinforced cavalry or combined-arms package:

- mounted infantry platoons
- mechanized or armored support section
- rotary wing lift and CASEVAC support
- reconnaissance and drone-enabled target development
- mortar, JTAC, and limited precision fires

Friendly playable scope should stay at platoon or company level per mission, with battalion activity represented through narrative support.

### Enemy force

The enemy should be organized in layers:

1. coastal smugglers and port security militias
2. urban fighters and technical-mounted quick reaction forces
3. rural insurgent detachments and training camps
4. Ahmad Umar's hardened inner-security element

This creates a natural escalation from irregular harassment to deliberate defensive stands.

## Campaign shape

### Phase I: coastal lodgment and urban break-in

Objectives:

- gain access to the littoral battlespace
- establish a foothold near port and airfield infrastructure
- disrupt coastal facilitators
- recover personnel and intelligence
- identify how the WMD network escapes inland

Recommended mission tone:

- fast raids
- combined-arms breach operations
- urban fringe fights
- high information value targets

### Phase II: inland pursuit and network reduction

Objectives:

- pursue retreating facilitators
- destroy camps and logistics nodes
- interdict cross-border movement
- recover WMD materials or precursor stockpiles
- isolate Ahmad Umar's command group

Recommended mission tone:

- wide-area mounted sweeps
- convoy security with deliberate raids
- air assault to block retreats
- reconnaissance-led objective reduction

### Finale: border stronghold encirclement

Objectives:

- seal the final sanctuary
- collapse remaining resistance
- capture or kill Ahmad Umar
- secure proof of the WMD network and its external ties

Recommended mission tone:

- multi-axis assault
- layered isolation and blocking positions
- limited urban reduction with clear end state

## Reforger-first mission line

The campaign should treat the original source missions as intent and remake them into Reforger-sized operations.

| Original mission | Reforger-first adaptation | Role in campaign |
| --- | --- | --- |
| M2 - Dealbreaker | Coastal or riverine raid against pirate-militia facilitators linked to Ahmad Umar | Opens the campaign, establishes the coastal network, seeds the WMD thread |
| M4 - Deliverance | Downed-aircraft rescue in hostile urban fringe terrain | Escalates the threat and forces entry into contested suburbs |
| M6 - Thunder and Lightening | Mechanized breakthrough to seize a military complex, highway node, and sustainment route | Creates the decisive shift from raid tempo to deliberate combined-arms operations |
| M8 - Window Shopping | Cordon and assault in market and industrial districts, ending with proof the HVT escaped inland | Closes Phase I and transitions the campaign to pursuit |
| M10 | Wide-area sweep to locate retreat routes, caches, and first interior logistics hubs | Opens Phase II |
| M12 | Raid or air assault on a rural camp complex tied to WMD transit | Builds operational pressure |
| M14 | Strike on a convoy, depot, or crossing point used to move HVT support personnel | Cuts the network apart |
| M16 | Recovery of WMD stockpile, precursor material, or technical documentation from a hardened site | Delivers the strategic payoff |
| M18 | Final encirclement and assault on Ahmad Umar's sanctuary | Campaign climax |

## First four mission specs

### M2 - Dealbreaker

**Mission type:** coastal raid  
**Terrain need:** littoral compound, fishing harbor or river mouth, nearby village, extract route  
**Playable force:** reinforced platoon with boats or helicopter insertion option  
**Core tasks:**

- seize a facilitator compound
- capture or kill a coastal broker
- recover manifests, radios, and route intel
- destroy small craft or weapons cache if time allows

**Why it fits Reforger:** supports short, replayable combined-arms action without overextending the first mission.

### M4 - Deliverance

**Mission type:** personnel recovery  
**Terrain need:** urban fringe crash site, dense blocks, rooftop threat positions, casualty collection point  
**Playable force:** quick reaction platoon with armored support and CASEVAC plan  
**Core tasks:**

- secure the crash site
- recover surviving aircrew
- hold long enough for extraction
- exploit wreckage or nearby safehouse for intelligence

**Why it fits Reforger:** creates a focused rescue mission with layered urban pressure and a clear extraction end state.

### M6 - Thunder and Lightening

**Mission type:** mechanized deliberate attack  
**Terrain need:** highway network, military compound, fuel or ammo point, defensible urban edge  
**Playable force:** company-minus with IFV or tank support, engineers, mortars  
**Core tasks:**

- breach outer defenses
- seize the complex and route junction
- repel counterattack
- establish the route for follow-on battalion movement

**Why it fits Reforger:** becomes the signature large-scale mission that validates the terrain-first campaign approach.

### M8 - Window Shopping

**Mission type:** urban cordon and assault  
**Terrain need:** market district, industrial lots, alleys, vehicle blocking positions, civilian clutter  
**Playable force:** reinforced platoon or company with multiple blocking elements  
**Core tasks:**

- isolate the district
- clear identified target structures
- detain facilitators and recover documents
- confirm Ahmad Umar escaped inland through a prepared corridor

**Why it fits Reforger:** closes the city phase with a dense but bounded objective and a clean transition to the interior campaign.

## Vertical-slice target

The first playable vertical slice should be **M2 - Dealbreaker**.

### Why M2 is the best slice

- it proves the new fictional theater without requiring the full campaign
- it tests littoral insertion, compound assault, intelligence pickup, and organized extraction
- it establishes the visual and gameplay language for the rest of the campaign

### Vertical-slice success criteria

The slice should demonstrate:

- one finished objective area on the primary coastal terrain
- one working insertion method and one extraction method
- mission briefing and clear commander intent
- scripted intelligence recovery that points to the wider network
- RHS-based force package, loadouts, and transport mix
- enemy alert and reinforcement behavior appropriate to Reforger player counts

### Systems the slice should validate

- assault staging and mission start flow
- transport timing
- objective triggers
- casualty handling and CASEVAC expectations
- extraction completion logic
- debrief that hands off naturally into M4

## Implementation priorities

1. Lock the fictional theater and terrain package.
2. Build the M2 vertical slice on the primary coastal terrain.
3. Carry the same faction package, briefing format, and mission scale into M4, M6, and M8.
4. Expand into the inland pursuit once the Phase I structure plays well in Reforger.

## Decision summary

The repository should move forward with a terrain-first, modern RHS, Reforger-native version of Juniper Spear.

The adaptation should preserve the campaign's structure and stakes while dropping the requirement to represent Mogadishu literally.
