# U.S. Military doctrine integration

## Purpose

This document anchors Campaign Juniper Spear 2026 to the U.S. Army and joint doctrine most relevant to its intended scope.

It is not meant to turn the project into a literal doctrinal simulation. It defines the doctrinal frame that should shape campaign design, mission planning, briefing products, and continuity decisions.

## Campaign-wide implications

### 1. The campaign is a battalion fight inside a joint campaign

The playable layer should remain platoon- and company-centered, but the narrative frame must consistently show the task force operating within a wider joint and partner-led campaign. Higher headquarters, theater ISR, partner security forces, and regional containment forces are not flavor; they are part of the doctrinal scope.

### 2. Every mission should use purpose, key tasks, and end state

Mission briefs, controller packets, handoffs, and AAR products should all preserve the basic doctrinal chain of:

- purpose
- key tasks
- end state
- shaping facts for the next operation

That keeps campaign continuity aligned with the operations process instead of reducing each mission to a self-contained firefight.

### 3. Secondary missions are doctrinal shaping operations

The campaign's odd-numbered missions are not filler. They are reconnaissance, security, planning, sustainment, civil, and condition-setting operations that support the next decisive action. Their value should be measured by how they improve or degrade the next primary mission's information, positioning, support posture, or legitimacy.

### 4. Urban missions must emphasize isolation and control

Urban combat should be framed around isolation, cordon, breach, controlled clearance, infrastructure seizure, and civilian-risk management. The campaign should avoid treating dense urban terrain as a simple attrition arena.

### 5. Stability tasks start early, not after the finale

Checkpoint work, MEDCAPs, detainee handling, partner-force handoff, information effects, and population control all belong inside the operational campaign, not only in the post-combat epilogue. This supports the existing Hadir Littoral legitimacy and civilian-density design.

### 6. WMD objectives are secure-site problems

WMD-linked missions should prioritize:

- isolation of the site
- positive control of personnel and materiel
- contamination-control assumptions
- technical exploitation and evidence preservation
- controlled handoff to higher or specialized echelons

The campaign should avoid presenting those sites as targets to simply demolish from standoff.

### 7. Sustainment and CASEVAC must stay visible

Expeditionary logistics, route security, casualty evacuation, and recovery should remain part of the campaign's scope because the doctrine baseline treats endurance and operational reach as combat multipliers, not background abstractions.

### 8. Unit-type doctrine should shape the playable fight

The playable layer is platoon and company, which means mission designers and controllers should work from the unit-type ATPs, not only the campaign-level FMs. In practice:

- Infantry missions should reflect ATP 3-21.8 battle drills, movement formations, and fire-and-maneuver techniques at the squad and platoon level.
- Mechanized and armored missions should use ATP 3-90.1 company team task organization, integrating mounted overwatch, breach support, and dismounted clearing as combined-arms actions rather than separate events.
- Reconnaissance and security missions should follow ATP 3-20.98 scout platoon methods for route reconnaissance, area reconnaissance, and screen operations.
- Mortar and fire support planning should be grounded in ATP 3-21.90 and ATP 3-09.42 so indirect fires and CAS are coordinated at the company team level, not ad hoc.
- Engineer tasks should be framed through ATP 3-34.22 so breaching, obstacle reduction, and route clearance are planned as integrated combined-arms actions.

This does not mean every session requires a tactics class. It means the mission designs, controller packets, and briefing products should look like the units are fighting the way their manuals describe rather than defaulting to generic shooter mechanics.

## Scope adjustments for current project documents

The rest of the repository should read as if the following are true:

1. **The campaign is a doctrinally grounded combined-arms task-force campaign, not just a mission anthology.**
2. **Primary missions are decisive operations; secondary missions are shaping operations.**
3. **Urban combat requires civilian control, infrastructure logic, and disciplined fires.**
4. **Civil engagement, checkpoint work, and legitimacy tasks are operationally meaningful.**
5. **WMD-linked targets require secure-site handling and evidence discipline.**
6. **Mission orders and continuity products should preserve purpose, tasks, end state, and assessed follow-on implications.**
7. **The playable fight is governed by unit-type ATPs — infantry platoon and squad, armored and mechanized company team, scout platoon, mortar, engineer, and fire support procedures — not only by campaign-level field manuals.**

## Recommended use inside this repository

- Use this document when refining campaign scope, rewriting mission concepts, or deciding whether a mission belongs in the line at all.
- Use it alongside the WARNO, campaign plan, ORBAT, and staff templates when drafting mission briefings or follow-on orders.
- Use the unit-type-to-ORBAT crosswalk to identify which tactical publication governs a given formation before designing its mission tasks, battle drills, or controller packet.
- Use it as the doctrine checklist for future OPORDs, controller packets, and continuity reviews.
