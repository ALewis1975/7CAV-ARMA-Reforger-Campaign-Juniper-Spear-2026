# Staff role playbooks

## Purpose

These playbooks split campaign work into smaller, trainable billets so continuity does not depend on a small number of senior staff.

## Mission commander

### Purpose

Own the mission's player-facing plan, command intent, and final decision authority during execution.

### Pre-mission checklist

- confirm mission purpose, key tasks, and end state
- approve the mission briefing
- review controller packet assumptions and branch triggers
- confirm task organization and succession of command

### During mission

- preserve command intent
- avoid unnecessary tempo inflation
- identify mission-level deviations that matter for canon

### Post-mission checklist

- provide a short commander's summary
- identify decisive moments and failures
- flag disputed outcomes for review

### Required handoff outputs

- commander summary for the intake packet
- priority corrections to the draft fact sheet

## Controller or GM

### Purpose

Protect pacing, friction, and branch discipline without replacing player decision-making.

### Pre-mission checklist

- review the controller packet and pace card
- confirm escalation rules and do-not-accelerate rules
- confirm branch triggers, fail-forward conditions, and stop conditions
- prepare injects and notes format for later review

### During mission

- let planned friction play out before escalating
- intervene only when needed to preserve intent, pacing, or mission coherence
- record each major controller intervention with time and reason

### Post-mission checklist

- summarize major interventions
- identify where controller actions diverged from plan
- recommend changes to future controller packets

### Required handoff outputs

- GM notes for the intake packet
- controller drift notes for review

## AAR curator

### Purpose

Collect and normalize the raw material needed for reliable post-mission synthesis.

### Pre-mission checklist

- prepare the mission intake packet shell
- confirm who will collect forum, casualty, logistics, and screenshot inputs
- publish deadlines for element AAR responses

### Post-mission checklist

- compile the forum AAR master post and element responses
- attach casualty, logistics, and controller notes
- identify missing or contradictory source material
- separate observed facts from unsupported claims

### Required handoff outputs

- completed intake packet
- source list with missing items called out

## Continuity officer *(AI agent)*

### Purpose

Protect canon, traceability, and consistency across missions. This billet is filled by an AI agent operating under human oversight. The agent drafts continuity assessments and ledger changes; campaign leadership retains final approval authority.

### Pre-mission checklist

- review the most recently approved ledger state
- identify continuity constraints that the next mission must honor
- flag pending review items that could affect the mission baseline

### Post-mission checklist

- review draft state deltas against the intake packet
- mark disputed facts and approval-gated changes
- ensure named actors, logistics, and unresolved threads remain consistent

### Required handoff outputs

- approved or rejected ledger changes
- continuity notes for the next planner

## Intel and order drafter *(AI agent)*

### Purpose

Turn approved facts into usable staff products with minimal rewrite effort. This billet is filled by an AI agent operating under human oversight. The agent drafts staff products from the approved ledger; campaign leadership retains final approval authority.

### Pre-mission checklist

- review the approved ledger and current planning guidance
- collect relevant open risks, leads, and named actors
- prepare briefing and order shells

### Post-mission checklist

- draft the fact sheet, OPSUM, intel annex, and next-mission handoff
- keep narrative outputs aligned to approved ledger state
- mark inference separately from observed fact

### Required handoff outputs

- draft staff products for review
- planner-ready next-mission handoff package

## Cross-role handoff standard

Every mission should leave behind:

- one intake packet
- one source-checked fact sheet
- one proposed state delta
- one reviewable PR for canon decisions
- one approved next-mission handoff package
