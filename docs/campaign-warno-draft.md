# Campaign WARNO Draft

## Purpose

This warning order reframes Campaign Juniper Spear as a single campaign-wide operations plan for ARMA Reforger.

It keeps the original campaign spine of coastal entry, urban fighting, inland pursuit, WMD recovery, and final HVT encirclement while shifting the setting into the fictional **Hadir Littoral** and **Daryan Interior** battlespaces.

## Header

- Operation: Juniper Spear 2026
- Order type: Warning order
- Echelon: Battalion campaign framework with platoon and company playable scope
- Supported format: ARMA Reforger combined-arms campaign
- Draft status: Planning baseline

## 1. Situation

### Operational context

Ahmad Umar leads an extremist network that combines militia fighters, pirate facilitators, smugglers, defected military personnel, and inland camp security forces. His network has used the Hadir coast for infiltration and maritime movement, the city for concealment and intimidation, and the interior for survival, dispersal, and sanctuary.

Current reporting indicates the network is attempting to move or conceal chemical or radiological material that could be turned into a strategic terror weapon or bargaining tool. The campaign must therefore move at a tempo that denies the enemy time to disperse the material, relocate key facilitators, or build political momentum through prolonged urban control.

### Friendly forces

- A reinforced cavalry or combined-arms task force is the main friendly formation.
- Playable forces remain platoon or company sized per mission.
- Battalion, brigade, JSOC, and host-nation pressure are represented through briefing context, support relationships, and follow-on reporting.
- Rotary wing lift, CASEVAC, reconnaissance, mortars, JTAC, and limited precision fires are available as mission-dependent support.

### Enemy forces

The enemy should be presented in four layers:

1. coastal smugglers, militia guards, and pirate-linked facilitators
2. urban fighters, technical-mounted quick reaction forces, and entrenched strongpoints
3. rural insurgent detachments, convoy escorts, and logistics camps
4. Ahmad Umar's hardened inner-security element and sanctuary guard force

### Terrain

- **Hadir Littoral:** port approaches, airfield infrastructure, peri-urban sprawl, dense city districts, and maneuver corridors for mounted and helicopter operations
- **Daryan Interior:** scrubland, desert lanes, sparse settlements, crossing sites, remote compounds, and pursuit corridors
- **Optional finale objective:** contained border-city district or hardened sanctuary complex if a third terrain is used

### Constraints

- The campaign must feel like a battalion fight without requiring brigade-scale playable density.
- Civilian presence remains significant in urban and market areas; restrictive ROE should be preserved.
- WMD-related sites must be handled as secure-site problems, not as indiscriminate destruction targets.
- Friendly tempo matters more than total terrain clearance; delay favors Umar.

### Assumptions

- Umar intends to preserve freedom of movement by abandoning exposed coastal nodes once they are compromised.
- Enemy command and control will be fragmented at the edges, but more cohesive around protected facilitators, depots, and final sanctuary objectives.
- Intelligence gained in each mission should narrow the next objective rather than fully reveal the campaign end state at once.

## 2. Mission

Task Force Juniper Spear conducts sequential combined-arms operations across the Hadir Littoral and Daryan Interior to seize the coastal lodgment, shatter Ahmad Umar's support network, recover WMD material or precursor stockpiles, and capture or kill Ahmad Umar before he can escape to a protected sanctuary.

## 3. Execution

### Commander's intent

- **Purpose:** prevent Umar's network from using WMD material, collapse his operational sanctuary, and restore freedom of action in the theater
- **Key tasks:** secure coastal access, disrupt facilitators, recover intelligence, destroy inland logistics nodes, isolate WMD handling sites, and finish the campaign with a deliberate encirclement
- **End state:** Umar's network is fragmented, WMD material is secured, major support zones are neutralized, and the final sanctuary is reduced or isolated beyond recovery

### Concept of the operation

The campaign runs in three broad phases.

- disrupt the coastal facilitation network

- confirm the enemy's inland escape pattern

### Campaign sequencing guidance

Each mission should hand the next one a clear continuity package:

- what was confirmed
- what remains unresolved
- which route, site, or person becomes the next focal point
- what capability the enemy lost
- what new risk the task force now carries

Campaign progression should feel cumulative. The enemy loses freedom of movement first, then logistics depth, then sanctuary, and only at the end loses the ability to preserve Umar.

### Secondary mission design guidance

Odd-numbered missions (M1, M3, M5, M7, M11, M13, M15, M17) are **secondary missions** designed for HQ and specialized task-force elements rather than the full maneuver force.

Secondary mission characteristics:

- **Smaller scale:** section to platoon playable force instead of company
- **Specialized task units:** Ranger, aviation, armor, medical, support, or HQ staff rather than combined-arms maneuver
- **Support role:** each secondary mission sets conditions for the next primary mission by providing intelligence, security, sustainment, or planning
- **Shorter duration:** designed for one- to two-hour play sessions with focused objectives
- **Canon contribution:** secondary mission outcomes feed the campaign continuity loop and may alter conditions for the next primary mission

Secondary missions run between primary missions in campaign chronology. A secondary mission that fails or is skipped should degrade the conditions for the next primary mission rather than block the campaign sequence entirely.

### Tasks to subordinate elements

#### Maneuver force

- clear and hold only the terrain necessary to preserve tempo and the next operation
- isolate built-up areas before entering them
- preserve assault strength for decisive objectives instead of overcommitting to low-value clearance

#### Reconnaissance and surveillance

- identify escape routes, reinforcement lanes, and hidden support zones
- confirm which recovered reporting is reliable enough to drive the next mission
- maintain pressure on possible Umar movement without forcing every mission to include direct HVT contact

#### Aviation

- support insertion, quick repositioning, CASEVAC, and blocking-force employment
- extend operational reach in the littoral and interior without replacing ground maneuver as the decisive action

#### Fires

- provide limited, deliberate support for breach, isolation, and counterattack defeat
- minimize collateral damage in urban zones and around WMD-sensitive sites

#### Sustainment elements

- support mobile resupply and casualty collection across separated objectives
- prepare for site exploitation, detainee handling, and contamination control at WMD-linked targets

### Coordinating instructions

- Every mission must reinforce the campaign spine of coast, city, interior, and sanctuary.
- Use battalion and brigade activity in the briefings to explain wider pressure beyond the playable area.
- Preserve civilian density and restrictive ROE in market, residential, and mixed-use zones.
- Build branch paths that change the next mission's conditions without breaking the campaign sequence.
- Favor fail-forward outcomes over hard campaign resets.

### Priority intelligence requirements

- Where is Umar's current command node or sanctuary link?
- Which facilitators are moving personnel, materiel, or WMD-related cargo?
- Which terrain corridor is still open to the enemy after each mission?
- What proof connects coastal smugglers, urban cells, and interior camps into one network?

## 4. Sustainment

- Sustainment should be represented as mobile, expeditionary, and limited rather than static and abundant.
- CASEVAC must remain a visible planning concern across both coastal and interior missions.
- WMD-site missions require secure-site procedures, contamination control, and controlled exploitation.
- Detainee handling, captured media, and recovered documents should feed the campaign continuity workflow.

## 5. Command and Signal

- Campaign command remains battalion-led with higher headquarters shaping the wider theater.
- Battalion command posts, forward support areas, and aviation support zones should move as the fight transitions from littoral to interior.
- Debriefs, OPSUMs, and handoff notes should be recorded using the repository reporting templates so mission outcomes can become campaign canon cleanly.

## Planning end state for this draft

This WARNO should serve as the campaign-level baseline from which individual mission OPORDs, intel annexes, and post-mission handoffs are derived.
