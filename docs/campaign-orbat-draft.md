# Campaign ORBAT Draft

## Purpose

This draft ORBAT provides a campaign-level force structure for the Reforger-first Juniper Spear adaptation.

It combines the original Juniper Spear source force package with the current fictional Hadir Littoral and Daryan Interior framing so mission briefs can reference a consistent set of friendly, partner, hostile, and variable-alignment actors.

## Draft assumptions

- This is a campaign design document, not a final canon roster.
- Playable scope remains platoon or company sized, with battalion and joint-force activity represented through briefing, radio traffic, off-map support, and follow-on consequences.
- U.S. higher headquarters and core cavalry formations stay recognizable from the original source material.
- Host-nation and regional actors are intentionally kept partly generic until the campaign lead locks the final state, faction names, and terrain package.
- Enemy structure follows the layered model established in the Reforger-first adaptation guide: coastal facilitators, urban fighters, rural detachments, and Ahmad Umar's inner security element.

## 3. Opposing-force ORBAT

### 3.1 Campaign-level hostile structure

| Formation | Type | Primary campaign role | Typical capabilities |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Ahmad Umar command network | Extremist command and control core | Directs the overall insurgent-smuggler coalition and controls strategic decisions around the WMD thread | Couriers, safe houses, propaganda apparatus, compartmented leadership |
| Coastal facilitation ring | Pirate-smuggler and militia network | Receives illicit cargo, moves personnel, and protects maritime routes | Skiffs, trucks, docks, warehouses, bribed officials, technicals |
| Hadir urban fighting groups | Irregular urban combat force | Hold districts, ambush relief forces, and shield leadership movement | Machine guns, RPGs, IEDs, snipers, dense urban strongpoints |
| Captured heavy weapons group | Defected military and seized equipment force | Provides the enemy's limited armored and mechanized punch | Captured APCs, armored vehicles, recoilless rifles, mortars, trained crews |
| Daryan interior detachments | Rural insurgent network | Camp security, route denial, cache protection, sanctuary management | Pickups, light mortars, recoilless rifles, dispersed camps |
| External facilitator cells | Black-market brokers and extremist liaisons | Funding, procurement, false documents, and movement across borders | Finance, contraband handling, corrupt intermediaries |
| Inner security detachment | Hardened HVT guard force | Protects Ahmad Umar, WMD sites, and final sanctuary nodes | Best fighters, better comms discipline, prepared fallback positions |

### 3.2 Hostile echelon breakdown by battlespace

| Battlespace | Main hostile actors | Notional size | Employment concept |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Littoral approaches | Coastal facilitators, port gunmen, small-craft smugglers | Squad to platoon packets | Delay coastal raids, hide manifests, buy time for inland movement |
| Port and peri-urban belt | Port militia, urban QRF technicals, compromised security personnel | Platoon to company packets | Defend depots, launch quick counterattacks, screen key personalities |
| Dense city districts | Urban fighters, propaganda/security cells, foreign fighter cadre | Company to battalion network | Fix friendly forces in alleys and compounds while leadership displaces |
| Interior road network | Rural detachments, convoy escorts, cache guards | Squad to platoon packets | Ambush convoys, protect crossing sites, preserve logistics flow |
| Camp complexes and hard sites | Training cadres, WMD handling cell, heavy-weapons guard | Platoon to company packets | Force deliberate assaults and create higher-stakes objective fights |
| Final sanctuary | Ahmad Umar inner guard, loyal veteran fighters, remaining heavy assets | Company-minus to battalion-minus | Conduct layered defense, deception, and breakout attempts |

### 3.3 Hostile subcomponents worth naming in briefs

| Subcomponent | Function in story and mission design |
| --- | --- |
| Port brokers | Connect pirate shipping, warehouse labor, and customs corruption |
| Technical QRF groups | Provide fast-moving reinforcement inside the city and on inland highways |
| Foreign fighter cadre | Adds pockets of better-trained resistance and propaganda value |
| Ex-military armor crews | Explain captured heavy equipment and more disciplined enemy defense |
| WMD transit cell | Handles movement, concealment, and technical security around strategic material |
| Media and intimidation wing | Broadcasts executions, threats, and false narratives to influence civilian behavior |

## 4. Variable-alignment and non-state actors

| Actor | Alignment | Campaign role | Use in mission design |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Local clan or district militias | Variable | Can support, resist, or hedge based on momentum and civilian impact | Useful for checkpoints, false tips, sudden route denial, or temporary allies |
| Commercial transport and dock syndicates | Opportunistic | Move cargo for whoever controls the port and road network | Enable smuggling, informant recruitment, or deception |
| Civilian police remnants | Mixed | Some cooperate with the government, some are intimidated or compromised | Adds uncertainty at checkpoints and detention sites |
| Aid groups and medical charities | Neutral | Highlight civilian risk, displacement, and legitimacy concerns | Raise ROE pressure and post-fight stabilization needs |
| Local media and religious figures | Variable | Shape information environment and public reaction | Useful for consequence tracking after high-profile actions |
